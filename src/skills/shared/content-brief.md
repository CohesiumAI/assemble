# Skill : Content Brief

## Objective

Produce a complete and actionable content brief that guides the writer from A to Z: editorial objective, target keywords, SERP competitive analysis, recommended structure, tone and style, and measurable success criteria. Each brief must enable the creation of SEO-performing content that engages the target audience.

## When to use

- When planning new content (article, landing page, guide)
- To brief an internal or external writer on a specific topic
- When existing content needs to be rewritten or significantly updated
- When creating an editorial calendar with detailed briefs
- To align the content team on the SEO and marketing objectives of a piece

## Steps

1. **Define the editorial objective** — Clarify the content's purpose: organic traffic acquisition, conversion, education, awareness, link building. Identify the target audience (persona), their maturity in the buying journey (awareness, consideration, decision), and the expected outcome after reading.
2. **Research and validate keywords** — Identify the primary keyword and secondary keywords (search volume, difficulty, trend). Analyze semantically related terms (NLP/TF-IDF). Verify positioning feasibility based on domain authority.
3. **Analyze search intent and the SERP** — Decode search intent (informational, navigational, transactional, commercial). Analyze the top 10 results: content type (listicle, guide, comparison), average length, structure, differentiating elements. Identify featured snippet opportunities.
4. **Analyze editorial competition** — Study the top 3-5 ranked content pieces: strengths (completeness, data, visuals), weaknesses (missing sections, dated content, lack of depth). Define the differentiating angle for the content to produce.
5. **Structure the content** — Propose a detailed outline with sections (H2/H3), key points to cover in each section, user questions to address (People Also Ask), data/statistics to include, recommended visuals (infographics, tables, screenshots).
6. **Define tone and writing guidelines** — Specify the tone (expert, conversational, educational), target reading level, recommended length, brand constraints, terms to use or avoid, CTA format.
7. **Establish performance criteria** — Define measurable KPIs: target position, expected organic traffic, estimated click-through rate, conversion objective, engagement metrics (time on page, scroll depth). Set the first evaluation date.

## Exit Checklist

- [ ] Editorial objective and target audience are clearly defined
- [ ] Primary and secondary keywords are validated (volume, difficulty)
- [ ] Search intent is identified and the content addresses it
- [ ] Competitive analysis reveals a clear differentiating angle
- [ ] Structure (H2/H3) covers all important sub-topics
- [ ] Tone, length, and writing guidelines are specified
- [ ] Performance criteria are measurable and realistic
- [ ] The brief is self-contained (a writer can produce the content without questions)
